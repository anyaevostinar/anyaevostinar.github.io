## Overview
In today's class, you will be showing off your final project to your peers! In preparation for that, you should make sure that you have a functioning program, even if it doesn't have all the bells and whistles you dream about. You should also be prepared to explain to them the idea of your project.

## Basic Learning Objectives
Before class, you should be able to:
* Run your project on the lab machines
* Explain your classes and meaningful interaction between objects

## Advanced Learning Objectives
After class, you should be able to:
* Update your project based on peer feedback
* Complete your final project and README


## Checks
1. Make sure that you have a functioning version of your project that you can access on the lab machines. You can put it in COURSES or email it to yourself.

2. Submit to Moodle a summary that you plan to tell to your peers about how they play your game and what the classes, objects, and meaningful interactions are in the game.
